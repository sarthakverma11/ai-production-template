Company Travel Policy

Company Business travel must be approved by the reporting manager before bookings are made. The travel request should explain the business purpose, destination, expected dates, and estimated cost. Employees should avoid unnecessary travel when a remote meeting is sufficient.

Company Travel booking should be completed through the approved company process or approved travel partner. Employees are expected to choose reasonable flight, train, or bus options based on safety, timing, and cost. Any change to travel dates should be approved by the manager.

Hotel reimbursement is available for approved business trips. Employees should choose standard business hotels within the budget shared by the finance team. Extra personal expenses, upgrades, or minibar charges are not reimbursable unless a manager has approved an exception.This is for all employees.

Food expenses during business travel may be reimbursed within the daily limit set by finance. Employees should keep meal expenses reasonable and related to the business trip. Alcohol and personal entertainment expenses are not covered by this policy.

Cab reimbursement is allowed for travel between home, office, airport, railway station, hotel, and client locations when it supports the approved trip. Employees should use safe and practical transport options. Local travel that is unrelated to the business purpose is not reimbursable.

Receipts are required for travel, hotel, food, and cab reimbursement claims. Employees must upload clear receipt images in the expense system and submit the claim within the required timeline. Claims without receipts may be rejected or returned for correction.
