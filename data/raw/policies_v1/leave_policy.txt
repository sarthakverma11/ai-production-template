Company Leave Policy

Employees receive twelve days of casual leave for each calendar year. Casual leave is intended for planned personal work, family commitments, or short breaks. Employees should request casual leave in advance through the HR portal so that managers can plan team coverage.

Sick leave is available when an employee is unwell or needs medical rest. Employees should inform their manager as early as possible and update the HR portal when they are able to do so. For longer absences, HR may request supporting medical information according to company policy.

Unused casual leave may be carried forward up to five days into the next calendar year. Leave above the carry-forward limit expires at the end of the year. Sick leave is tracked separately and should be used only for health-related needs.

All planned leave requires manager approval before the leave dates begin. The HR portal is the official system for submitting leave requests, checking balances, and viewing approval status. A leave request should include the dates, type of leave, and a short reason.

Emergency leave may be used when an employee faces an urgent personal situation and cannot request approval in advance. The employee should notify the manager by phone or message as soon as possible. After returning to work, the employee must update the HR portal so records remain complete.
